## Brain vs Body

- **Bot bridge (the Body)** — Built with **`createBotBridge`**. It owns the **Discord connection** and keeps **The Connection** open for your apps. Think of it as the chest that keeps the **heart** beating.
- **App (the Brain)** — Built with **`connectBotBridge`**. This is **your** logic: commands, dashboards, workers. You can **restart it**, **deploy it**, or **run several** (with separate secrets) without tearing down Discord.

<Callout type="tip" title="Analogy">

**Shardwire** is the **Heart** that stays beating while you swap the **Brain**. Restart the app; the gateway does not need to reconnect.

</Callout>

## The Connection

Between **Body** and **Brain** is **The Connection** (a WebSocket to the bridge). You mostly think in terms of:

- **Events** — “Something happened on Discord” (normalized objects you subscribe to with **`app.on`**).
- **Actions** — “Do something on Discord” (typed calls on **`app.actions.*`** that return **`result.ok`**).

You do **not** need to master transport details to ship your first bot—treat **The Connection** as “how my app talks to the bridge.”

## Bridge modes

- **`split`** (default): bot process + app process over WebSocket.
- **`hybrid`**: split mode plus optional access to the underlying discord.js client via **`bot.client()`**.
- **`single-process`**: no WebSocket transport; call **`bot.app()`** to get an in-process app bridge.

`single-process` is ideal for small bots or local migration stages, then you can move to split mode later without rewriting app handlers.

## Secrets: the password for your bridge

**Secrets** are how the bridge knows **which app is allowed**. You configure one or more secret strings on the **bot** side; each **app** presents the same secret (or a **scoped** secret with tighter permissions—advanced).

<Callout type="info" title="Beginner mental model">

Think of a secret as a **password** to your bridge—not your Discord token. Never commit secrets; use env vars in dev and your host’s secret store in production.

</Callout>

## Local vs internet

- On your laptop, **The Connection** often uses **`ws://`** to **localhost** only.
- On a server or two separate hosts, you use **`wss://`** (encrypted), like **`https://`** for WebSockets.

<Callout type="warn" title="Loopback rule">

**`ws://`** is for loopback (**127.0.0.1**, **localhost**) only. Anything else must use **`wss://`**. See [**Keeping it alive**](/docs/guides/keeping-it-alive) for deployment.

</Callout>
