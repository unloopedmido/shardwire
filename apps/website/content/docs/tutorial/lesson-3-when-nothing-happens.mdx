---
title: 'Lesson 3 — When nothing happens'
description: 'Treat “connected but quiet” as a capability audit: intents, secrets, subscriptions, and negotiated surface.'
---

Most confusing failures are **not** random WebSocket bugs. They are **mismatched expectations** between what your bot can emit, what your secret allows, and what your app subscribed to.

## 1. Handlers never run

Work through this list in order:

1. **`app.capabilities().events`** — is the event you subscribed to actually listed?
2. **Bot intents** on `createBotBridge` — missing intents silently remove events from negotiation ([Bot bridge](/docs/guides/bot-bridge)).
3. **Scoped secrets** — if you narrowed `allow.events`, the event must be permitted for that secret ([Capabilities & scoped secrets](/docs/concepts/capabilities-and-scopes)).
4. **Subscription name** — typo in `app.on('messageCreate', …)`?

## 2. Actions return `ok: false`

1. Branch on `result.ok` and log `result.error.code` ([App bridge](/docs/guides/app-bridge)).
2. Confirm the action appears in negotiated capabilities (not just your local wish list).

## 3. Strict startup surprises

<Callout type="error" title="Register handlers before `ready({ strict: true })`">

If you call `app.on(...)` **after** `await app.ready({ strict: true, … })`, strict startup cannot see those subscriptions and startup may fail or your contract will not match reality. Register handlers first, then call `ready`.

</Callout>

See [Strict startup](/docs/guides/strict-startup) and [Incremental adoption](/docs/guides/incremental-adoption) when you add manifests.

## 4. Still stuck

Use [Troubleshooting](/docs/operations/troubleshooting) and [Diagnostics](/docs/operations/diagnostics) — treat it as a **capability audit first**, then transport.

You now have a working local bridge. When you ship beyond loopback, continue with [Deployment](/docs/operations/deployment) and [Remote bridge (wss)](/docs/operations/remote-bridge).
